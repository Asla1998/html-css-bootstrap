KASARGOD


Kasaragod is the northernmost district of Kerala and is also known as Saptha Bhasha Sangama Bhoomi (The land of seven languages) as seven languages namely, Malayalam, Tulu, Kannada, Marathi, Konkani, Beary, and Urdu are spoken, unlike the other districts of Kerala.


Destination

Top Destinations

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is a medieval fort built by Shivappa Nayaka of Keladi in 1650 AD, at Bekal. It is the largest fort in Kerala, spreading over 40 acres.

Malik Deenar Juma Masjid

Malik Dinar Mosque is the second oldest mosque in India, situated in Thalangara in Kasaragod town of Kerala state, India.

Kareem Forest Park

Kareem's forest now produces hundreds of litres of water, saving an entire village from severe drought during summers.

Chandragiri Fort

Chandragiri Fort is a fort built in the 17th century, situated in Kasaragod District of Kerala, South India

Mallikarjuna Temple

Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Temple or Srisailam Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the deities Shiva and Parvati, located at Srisailam.

Shree Ananthapadmanabha

This is the only lake temple in Kerala and is believed to be the original seat of Ananthapadmanabha Swami Thiruvananthapuram.

Sea View Park

Awesome place for a refreshment with the beautiful views of The Arabian Sea, Chandragiri back water and Mangrove forests.

Kasaba Beach Park

This amazing garden is one of the most beautiful gardens in Kerala. On the eastern side of the garden, visitors will find a majestic river.

Madhur Sri Madanantheshwara

Madhur Sree Madanantheshwara-Siddhivinayaka Temple is a popular Shiva and Ganapathi temple located 7 km from Kasaragod town.
